🏥 Mental Health First Aid Kit

Recognizing, responding to, and supporting mental health needs with compassion

Our Mission: Provide accessible, stigma-free mental health first aid education that combines professional guidance with spiritual support for comprehensive care.
Important Disclaimer: This tool provides educational information and spiritual support for mental health first aid situations. It is not a substitute for professional medical care, therapy, or psychiatric treatment. Always encourage professional help for mental health concerns and call emergency services for immediate safety risks.

🚨 Mental Health Emergency

If someone is in immediate danger of harming themselves or others:

🇺🇸 Call 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline)
🚨 Call 911 for immediate safety risks

Outside the US: Use your local emergency number

Trust your instincts. When in doubt, seek immediate professional help.

👥 Recognizing Mental Health Concerns in Others

Learn to identify warning signs and know when and how to reach out with care

⚠️ General Warning Signs to Watch For

Significant changes in mood, behavior, or personality
Withdrawal from friends, family, or usual activities
Dramatic decline in work or school performance
Changes in sleep patterns (too much or too little)
Changes in appetite or eating habits
Increased use of alcohol or drugs
Neglecting personal hygiene or appearance
Expressing feelings of hopelessness or worthlessness
Talking about death, dying, or suicide
Giving away prized possessions
Extreme mood swings

🆘 Mental Health Crisis Response

Immediate protocols for responding to mental health emergencies

🚨 When to Call for Immediate Help

Call 911 or emergency services immediately if someone:

  • Has attempted suicide or self-harm
  • Is threatening to hurt themselves or others
  • Has a specific suicide plan
  • Is experiencing psychosis (seeing/hearing things that aren't there)
  • Is severely intoxicated and at risk
  • Is in immediate physical danger

📋 Crisis Response Protocol

Stay Calm and Present
Your calm presence can help stabilize the situation. Take deep breaths and remain focused.
Ensure Immediate Safety
Remove any means of self-harm if possible. Stay with the person or ensure they're not alone.
Listen Without Judgment
Let them talk. Don't try to fix everything or minimize their feelings. Validate their pain.
Ask Direct Questions
"Are you thinking about hurting yourself?" "Do you have a plan?" Be direct but compassionate.
Connect to Professional Help
Call 988, 911, or take them to an emergency room. Don't leave them alone during a crisis.
Follow Up
Continue to check in after the immediate crisis. Recovery is an ongoing process.

🤝 Ongoing Mental Health Support

Long-term strategies for supporting mental health recovery and wellness

🌱 How to Provide Ongoing Support

Educate Yourself
Learn about their specific mental health condition. Understanding reduces stigma and improves support.
Maintain Regular Contact
Check in consistently but respect boundaries. Small gestures of care matter greatly.
Encourage Professional Treatment
Support their engagement with therapy, medication, or other professional interventions.
Practice Patience
Recovery is not linear. There will be good days and difficult days. Stay consistent in your support.
Take Care of Yourself
Supporting someone with mental health challenges can be emotionally taxing. Seek your own support.
Create a Support Network
Help them build connections with others who understand and can provide various types of support.

🏠 Creating a Supportive Environment

Maintain open, non-judgmental communication
Establish predictable routines when possible
Encourage healthy habits (sleep, exercise, nutrition)
Reduce stressors where possible
Celebrate small victories and progress
Respect their autonomy and choices
Include them in social activities (without pressure)
Learn their warning signs and triggers

💚 The Ministry of Presence
